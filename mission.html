James Web Space Telescope

The James Webb Space Telescope (JWST)
is a powerful space observatory slated to
launch on December 18, 2021. Positioned
at the second Lagrange point, JWST boasts
a 6.5-meter mirror for infrared observations,
enabling groundbreaking studies of distant galaxies, star formation, and exoplanet atmospheres. Equipped with international collaboration from NASA, ESA, and CSA, JWST's tennis court-sized sunshield shields its instruments from the Sun, Earth, and Moon, ensuring accurate and advanced astronomical observations.

Hubble Space Telescope

The Hubble Space Telescope (HST) was
launched in 1990 to capture high-resolution
images of space. It orbits Earth, avoiding
atmospheric interference. Hubble has made groundbreaking discoveries, like measuring the universe's expansion rate. It's been serviced to extend its life. Its iconic images have popularized astronomy. Hubble remains in operation, with its successor, the James Webb Space Telescope, on the horizon.

Sputnic 1

Sputnik 1, launched in 1957, was the first,
artificial satellite, marking the start of the
space age. It was a small sphere with radio
signals, its launch initiated the space race
between the United States and the Soviet Union. It reentered Earth's atmosphere and burned up after three months in orbit.
