Information

MERCURY

Distance from the Sun: About 57.9 million kilometers (36 million miles). Characteristics: Mercury is the smallest planet and has extreme temperature variations, with scorching hot days and frigid nights. It has a very thin atmosphere and a heavily cratered surface.

VENUS

Distance from the Sun: Approximately 108.2 million kilometers (67.2 million miles). Characteristics: Venus is similar in size and composition to Earth but has a thick, toxic atmosphere primarily composed of carbon dioxide. It experiences a runaway greenhouse effect, making its surface incredibly hot.

EARTH

Distance from the Sun: About 149.6 million kilometers (93 million miles). Characteristics: Earth is the only planet known to support life. It has a diverse climate, liquid water, and a variety of ecosystems. It's the third planet from the Sun and has a unique blend of geological and atmospheric conditions.

MARS

Distance from the Sun: Approximately 227.9 million kilometers (141.6 million miles). Characteristics: Mars is often called the "Red Planet" due to its reddish appearance. It has a thin atmosphere primarily composed of carbon dioxide. Mars has been a target for exploration with rovers and landers searching for signs of past or present life.

JUPITER

Distance from the Sun: About 778.3 million kilometers (483.8 million miles). Characteristics: Jupiter is the largest planet in our solar system and is composed mainly of hydrogen and helium. It has a strong magnetic field and numerous moons. The Great Red Spot is a famous storm on Jupiter's surface.

SATURN

Distance from the Sun: Approximately 1.4 billion kilometers (886 million miles). Characteristics: Saturn is known for its stunning ring system, which is made up of ice particles and rocky debris. It's the second-largest planet in our solar system and primarily consists of hydrogen and helium.

URANUS

Distance from the Sun: About 2.9 billion kilometers (1.8 billion miles). Characteristics: Uranus is an ice giant with a unique featureâ€”its axis of rotation is tilted nearly 90 degrees, causing it to roll on its side as it orbits the Sun. It has a bluish color due to its atmosphere containing methane.

NEPTUNE

Distance from the Sun: Approximately 4.5 billion kilometers (2.8 billion miles). Characteristics: Neptune is another ice giant and the farthest known planet from the Sun. It has a deep blue color, driven by the presence of methane in its atmosphere. It has a strong system of winds and a dynamic atmosphere.

SUN

Distance from Earth: Approximately 149.6 million kilometers (93 million miles). Characteristics: Central star of our solar system, hydrogen and helium composition, nuclear fusion, source of light and heat for Earth, 4.6 billion years old, critical for Earth's climate and ecosystem, influences space weather.

MOON

Distance from Earth: Approximately 384,400 kilometers (238,855 miles). Characteristics: Earth's natural satellite, cratered surface, plains, mountains, impact basins, thin exosphere, lacks a substantial atmosphere, extreme temperature variations, plays a crucial role in Earth's tides, subject of scientific study and exploration for centuries, humans have landed on its surface during Apollo missions.

TITAN

Distance from Saturn: Approximately 1.2 million kilometers (746,000 miles). Characteristics: Largest moon of Saturn, thick nitrogen atmosphere, orange haze, diverse landscape, liquid methane lakes, organic molecules.
